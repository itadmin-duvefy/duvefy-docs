## Overview

Every user in an organization has one of three roles: **Owner**, **Admin**, or **Member**. Roles control what a user can see and do across the platform.

---

## Role comparison

| Permission | Owner | Admin | Member |
|-----------|:-----:|:-----:|:------:|
| View conversations & analytics | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| View agents | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Create / edit / delete agents | Yes | Yes | — |
| Configure knowledge base | Yes | Yes | — |
| Configure CRM integrations | Yes | Yes | — |
| Configure Stripe payments | Yes | Yes | — |
| Configure LLM (BYOK) | Yes | Yes | — |
| Configure SMTP settings | Yes | Yes | — |
| Invite new **members** | Yes | Yes | — |
| Remove members | Yes | Yes | — |
| Invite new **admins** | Yes | — | — |
| Change a member's role to Admin | Yes | — | — |
| Remove admins | Yes | — | — |
| View audit logs | Yes | Yes | — |

---

## Owner

The organization owner is the primary account holder, typically the first person invited when the organization was created.

Owners have full control over everything, including the ability to promote members to admin and demote admins back to members. Only one active owner exists per organization at a time.

<Note>
  Ownership can be transferred by Duvefy's operations team if needed.
</Note>

---

## Admin

Admins are power users who can configure most of the platform. They can:
- Create and manage agents and topics
- Connect CRM and payment integrations
- Set up LLM and SMTP settings
- Invite and remove **members** (but not other admins)

Admins cannot manage other admins or change the owner.

---

## Member

Members have read-only access. They can:
- View all conversations and message history
- View analytics on the dashboard
- View agents (but cannot edit them)

Members cannot change any configuration.

<Tip>
  Members are ideal for customer success, sales, or support staff who need visibility into conversations without the ability to change agent behavior.
</Tip>

---

## Changing roles

Owners can change a user's role from **Settings → Team**:
1. Find the team member in the list
2. Click the role dropdown next to their name
3. Select the new role and confirm

Role changes take effect immediately.
