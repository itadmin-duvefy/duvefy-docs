## What Salesforce enables

When connected, your agents can:
- Create and update any standard Salesforce object (Lead, Contact, Opportunity, Case, etc.)
- Query records to personalize conversations
- Trigger Salesforce Autolaunched Flows (Scale+)
- Call Apex REST class methods (Scale+)
- Send emails via the Salesforce `SingleEmailMessage` API (Scale+)

---

## Connecting Salesforce

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings → Integrations">
    In the sidebar, click **Settings**, then **Integrations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect Salesforce">
    Click the **Connect Salesforce** button on the Salesforce card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize in Salesforce">
    You'll be redirected to Salesforce's OAuth login page. Sign in with a Salesforce user that has API access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant permissions">
    Review the requested permissions and click **Allow**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to Duvefy">
    You'll be redirected back to the Integrations page. The Salesforce card will show a green connected status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Required Salesforce permissions

The Salesforce user used to connect must have:
- API Enabled permission
- Read/Create/Edit access to the objects your topics will interact with (e.g., Lead, Contact, Opportunity)
- For Flows: permission to invoke the specific Autolaunched Flows
- For Apex: permission to access the Apex REST classes

<Tip>
  Create a dedicated integration user in Salesforce with only the permissions needed. Avoid using a personal admin account.
</Tip>

## Disconnecting Salesforce

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings → Integrations">
    Click **Settings** → **Integrations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Disconnect">
    Click **Disconnect** on the Salesforce card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    A red confirmation panel appears inline. Click **Yes, disconnect** to confirm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Disconnecting Salesforce removes the stored access and refresh tokens. Any topics that rely on Salesforce will fail until you reconnect. You will need to re-authorize after reconnecting.
</Warning>
